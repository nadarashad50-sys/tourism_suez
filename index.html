Attractions

Discover the best of Suez

From family beaches and waterfront walks to the national museum, start your journey with these highlights.

Beach

Suez Beaches

Relax on local beaches facing the Gulf of Suez, with calm water and easy access for families.

Promenade

Port Tawfiq Promenade

A scenic waterfront walk where the canal meets the sea – perfect for sunset and photography.

Museum

Suez National Museum

A modern museum telling the story of Suez from ancient trade routes to the modern canal.

Heritage & History

Walk through living history

Suez has always been a gateway city – with mosques, villas and war sites that witnessed key moments.

Spiritual

Al-Ghareeb Mosque

One of the city’s spiritual hearts and a symbol closely tied to Suez identity.

Heritage

Canal & war stories

Monuments and open-air sites recall the October War and the role of Suez and its people.

Old Suez

Old waterfront houses

Historic façades and villas that overlook the sea and the old harbor area.

Stories from Suez

Taste, listen and explore

Discover local seafood, everyday life on the corniche and the stories told by the people of Suez.

Food

Fresh seafood by the gulf

Enjoy simple, authentic seafood restaurants with views of the water and passing ships.

Culture

Everyday canal stories

Listen to stories about ships, workers and families who built their lives around the canal.

Plan your trip

Practical information

Easy access from Cairo and nearby cities makes Suez ideal for a one-day escape or a full weekend.

Getting there

About 1.5 hours by car from Cairo via Suez Road, with buses and organized day trips available.

Best season

October to April offer comfortable weather for outdoor walks, festivals and sea views.

Suggested day

Morning at the museum, afternoon heritage walk, then sunset on the promenade and dinner by the sea.
